Safety

Overview

Whitewater rafting is an adventure, but safety always comes first. Before you start your adventures, understanding your gear helps you stay safe and confident while on the river, and avoid any dangerous scenarios which would harm you.

Required Safety Gear

Wear PFD (Life Jacket)

A properly fitted lifejacket is required for every rafter. It keeps you afloat if you fall in, and can save you from steep currents.

Wear Helmet

Helmets protect your head from impacts with paddles, rocks, or the raft itself. Helmets must remain fastened at all times for the best chances to be effective.

Wear Cold-Water Gear

In colder temperatures, wetsuits or splash jackets help maintain warmth and provide protection if you get splashed or fall in.

On-River Safety Rules

  • Always listen to your guide’s instructions.
  • If you fall in, float on your back with your feet pointed downstream.
  • Keep your head up to spot obstacles and signals.
  • Never stand up in moving water as it brings risk of foot entrapment.
  • Stay calm and follow rescue commands.

Pre-Trip Safety Checklist

  • Check that your life jacket and helmet are properly fitted.
  • Hydrate before your trip begins.
  • Apply sunscreen and secure sunglasses with a strap.
  • Ask questions if unsure about any instructions.
  • Listen carefully to the pre-trip safety briefing.
