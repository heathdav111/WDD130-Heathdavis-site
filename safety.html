Safety

Overview

Whitewater rafting is an adventure, but safety always comes first. Our guides are trained professionals who ensure that every guest understands the essential safety gear, rules, and procedures before entering the river. Whether you are new to rafting or returning for another trip, reviewing these guidelines helps keep everyone safe.

Required Safety Gear

Wear PFD (Life Jacket)

A properly fitted personal flotation device (PFD) is required for every rafter. It keeps you afloat and helps you maintain a safe body position if you enter the water.

Wear Helmet

Helmets protect your head from accidental impacts with rocks, paddles, or the raft itself. They must remain fastened at all times.

Wear Cold-Water Gear

Depending on water temperature, wetsuits or splash jackets help maintain warmth and prevent cold-related injuries in the event of an unexpected swim.
